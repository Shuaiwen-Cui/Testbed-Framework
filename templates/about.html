Wireless sensing system

SHM systems were built with wired sensor networks, but the great reliability and low installation and maintenance costs of WSNs have made them a promising alternative platform. The huge cost savings from deploying WSNs for SHM would allow them to be used in critical public and private infrastructure, increasing the adoption of applications such as short-term structural monitoring. Such systems increase the lifespan of many facilities by detecting problems early, reduce the cost of routine inspections, and, most importantly, improve public safety.

Raspberry pi

The raspberry pi does the necessary computations to determine the SHM status, including determining the position and extent of any damage. The Raspberry Pi serves as a hub for data collection from several NRF transceivers positioned throughout the building. Using a Wi-Fi module, the Raspberry Pi will transfer the data to the cloud. The data will be saved in the cloud and accessible from any mobile device. The technology has been validated in the lab using a real test bed.

Cloud database

Acceleration at a sampling rate of 100-500 Hz is the principal data source for SHM. This sample rate generates significant amounts of data, especially when numerous sensors are recorded on a structure. A cloud-SHM data sharing website was created to enable data synchronisation between smartphone and website, as well as data uploading and sharing, which can improve monitoring and big data integration efficiency, as well as the possibility of big data collection and quick structural safety evaluation.
